ETFs
Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs) are investment funds traded on stock exchanges, much like stocks. They hold assets such as stocks, commodities, or bonds and generally operate with an arbitrage mechanism designed to keep trading close to its net asset value.

Common categories for classifying ETFs
    Category Description
    Equity ETFs ETFs that invest in stocks, aiming to replicate the performance of a specific index.
    Bond ETFs ETFs that invest in bonds, providing exposure to various fixed-income securities.
    Commodity ETFs ETFs that invest in commodities such as gold, silver, or oil.
    Sector and Industry ETFs ETFs that focus on specific sectors or industries, such as technology or healthcare.
    International ETFs ETFs that invest in international markets, providing exposure to global economies.
