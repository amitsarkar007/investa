Frequently Asked Questions

What are government bonds?
Government bonds are debt securities issued by a government to raise capital. When you buy a government bond, you are essentially lending money to the government in exchange for periodic interest payments and the return of the principal amount at maturity.
How do I buy government bonds?
Buying government bonds typically involves participating in bond auctions or purchasing them through financial institutions, brokers, or government bond platforms. You can contact our investment advisors for guidance on the buying process.
Are government bonds a safe investment?
Government bonds are generally considered safe investments because they are backed by the government's ability to tax and print money. However, like any investment, they are not entirely risk-free. It's important to assess your risk tolerance and financial goals before investing.
