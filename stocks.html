Common Categories for Classifying Stocks

Category Description
Market Capitalization
  • Large-Cap Stocks: Over $10 billion.
  • Mid-Cap Stocks: Between $2 billion and $10 billion.
  • Small-Cap Stocks: Under $2 billion.
Sector or Industry Grouped by the industry or sector of operation.
Growth vs. Value
  • Growth Stocks: High growth in revenue and earnings.
  • Value Stocks: Undervalued relative to fundamentals.
Dividend Yield
  • Dividend Stocks: Regular dividend distribution.
  • Non-Dividend Stocks: Reinvest earnings.
Geographic Location Categorized by the company's geographic operations.
Blue-Chip Stocks Shares of well-established, financially stable companies.
Cyclical vs. Defensive
  • Cyclical Stocks: Tied to the economic cycle.
  • Defensive Stocks: Perform well during downturns.
Risk Profile Categorized by risk level.
Penny Stocks vs. Blue-Chip Stocks
  • Penny Stocks: Low-priced and speculative.
  • Blue-Chip Stocks: Established and stable.
International Stocks Companies based in other countries.
